# Escalation Rules for Support Tickets

## Summary

Support agents should escalate tickets when the request requires approval, specialized investigation, or a decision outside standard support authority.

## Standard Handling

Escalate tickets involving enterprise contracts, legal threats, compensation, repeated billing disputes, privacy requests, suspected security issues, data loss, confirmed service incidents, or customers asking for exceptions to policy.

Before escalation, agents should collect the customer account, order ID if relevant, timeline of events, screenshots or logs, customer impact, and the specific decision needed.

## Customer-Safe Wording

"I am going to escalate this to the appropriate team so we can review the details carefully. I will include the information you provided and follow up when we have the next update."

## Escalation

Use the escalation queue for urgent customer impact, the finance queue for billing disputes, the privacy/legal queue for regulated requests, and the engineering queue for confirmed technical defects.
